popular attractions






Pike Place Market

Pike Place Market is the oldest continuously operating farmers market in the country. The multilevel space is home to more than 500 shops, vendors, restaurants, and bars as well as a renovated public plaza with views of Puget Sound and the Olympic Mountains.





Seattle Great Wheel

This ferris wheel sits on Seattle's waterfront over the Puget Sound on pier 57 near Pike Place Market and Seattle Aquarium. From the top there is a fantastic view of the city, sound and mountains. The wheel is the only one of its kind to be built over water.





The Space Needle

A treasured Seattle icon, the Space Needle continues to symbolize the innovative and forward-thinking spirit of Seattle. The tower’s 520’ saucer-shaped “top house” offers visitors Seattle’s only 360-degree indoor and outdoor panoramic views of downtown, Mount Rainier, Puget Sound, and the Cascades and Olympic mountain ranges.





The Gum Wall

A brick wall covered in used chewing gum, located in an alleyway in Post Alley under Pike Place Market in downtown, the Market Theater Gum Wall is a Seattle landmark. When you visit, make sure to leave your mark by adding a piece of gum!
